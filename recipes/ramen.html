Tonkotsu Ramen

Description

This ramen noodle soup recipe is a delicious take on a Japanese classic. "Tonkotsu", which literally translates to "pork-bone", is often characterized by a creamy, rich broth and the inclusion of pork belly. It is certainly an upgrade from the "instant noodle"-style ramen many of us are all-too familiar with!

Steps

  1. Boil the pork bones in water for 5 minutes and then let simmer for 12 hours. Add some salt. You do have salt, right?
  2. Remove the pork bones. You now have pork bone water. Or as it's more commonly known in the soup world, broth.
  3. Boil noodles in your newly-created pork water until they are cooked but not soggy.
  4. Move into a bowl and throw some of those mushrooms and green onions into it because it makes you feel fancy.
  5. Oh my gosh, we forgot to make the pork belly. Cut it into thin slices and cook it like bacon. Then throw that on top of your soup.
  6. Sprinkle your sesame seeds on top.
